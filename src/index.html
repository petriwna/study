Student Life in Canada

International students need health insurance, housing, a bank account, and local transportation. Life on campus is enriching but requires adaptation. More at Universities Canada.

  • Health Insurance: Each province has different coverage rules. Some require private insurance, while others may provide public options.
  • Housing: Options include on-campus residences, homestays, or private rentals. Prices vary by city and location.
  • Banking: Opening a student bank account is easy and many banks offer no-fee options for newcomers.
  • Transportation: Students often benefit from discounted transit passes and can use buses, subways, or bikes depending on the city.
  • Student Support: Campuses offer support services like mental health counselling, academic help, and student groups to ease integration.

FAQ

Is IELTS always required to study in Canada?

No, some institutions accept alternative tests such as TOEFL or Duolingo English Test. In some cases, proof of previous education in English may suffice.

Can I work while studying in Canada?

Yes, international students with a valid study permit can work up to 20 hours per week during academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks.

What if my visa application is refused?

If your study permit is refused, you can review the refusal letter, correct the issues (e.g., incomplete documentation), and reapply. Itâ€™s important to demonstrate strong ties to your home country and sufficient funds.

Where can I find scholarships for studying in Canada?

You can search for scholarships on official platforms such as ScholarshipsCanada and through specific universities or provincial programs.
